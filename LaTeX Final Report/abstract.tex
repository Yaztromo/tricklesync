\begin{abstract}[\normalfont\large\bfseries]
The process of synchronizing information between a mobile computing device (such as a mobile phone, music player, or PDA) has remained relatively static since the mid 1990's.  Synchronization is still generally a user-initiated operation, often requiring the user to physically plug the device to be synchronized to the computer system they wish to replicate against.  At the same time and increasing number of such devices provide one or more wireless connectivity options, providing many new possibilities for automated, intelligent synchronization.  This thesis presents one such automated intelligent synchronization system, the Expedient Trickle Sync (ETS) algorithm, and details its development, method of evaluation, and results.
\end{abstract}
